# What is DanNet?
DanNet is a free, online dictionary of Danish that maps out how words relate to each other. You can see that "hund" (dog) is a kind of animal, that it connects to "hundehus" and "pote," and so on. It's built on decades of Danish language research and available at [wordnet.dk][home], where you can search and explore these connections visually.

## How to use DanNet
Click the **looking glass** in the upper left corner of the page to search for a Danish word. You will see a page showing the different meanings of the word, along with a diagram that shows how it connects to other words. You can click on any related word to explore further.

## Who made DanNet?
DanNet was created by the [Centre for Language Technology][CST] at the University of Copenhagen and [Dansk Sprog- og Litteraturselskab][DSL]. This edition was first published in 2023, supported by funding from the [Carlsberg Foundation][Carlsbergfondet].

## What can I do with the data?
All of DanNet's data is freely available under the [CC BY-SA 4.0][license] license. You can explore it on this website, or download it in various formats from the [about page][about].
